About Me

I'm a systems and software engineer who's been in the industry for the last decade working in both Windows and Linux. I've provided solutions for the government, healthcare, defense, marketing automation, and financial industries. A firm believer in learning one level below any problem that I need to solve, I've worked with most major programming languages, networking, cloud providers, and at the operating system level in order to find a solution with proper trade-offs for the task at hand.

Outside of the aformentioned work, I also served in the US military for a total of 12 years. The first six were as a member of the US Army where I had primarily focused on munitions logistics with brief stints in theatre-level intelligence and end-user desktop support. After my first enlistment, I joined the US Air Force where I worked with radio frequency systems leading frequency management, equipment inventory, solution engineering, as well as compliance with regulations from federal, military, and international entities.

On the personal side, I have a love of learning new things and tackling technical topics where I don't have previous expertise. My general preference is going as low-level as possible with a lot of my time spent looking at game development, operating systems, graphics programming, language implementations, and offensive security.
